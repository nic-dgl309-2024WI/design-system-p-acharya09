Conifers

"Conifers" refer to a division of vascular plants (plants with a system of vessels for conducting fluids) that includes cone-bearing trees and shrubs. These plants are characterized by their needle-like or scale-like leaves and typically bear cones that contain seeds. Pine Spruces Firs Cedars Junipers Cypresses

Outback Nursery

Outback Nursery is one of the largest garden centres on Vancouver Island. Our nursery is situated on a 6 acre property and offers 5 overflowing greenhouses (30x170ft); almost 23,000 square feet.

Outback Nursery began in 1998-1999 when our parents retired to the island from Aldergrove. They asked if we'd be interested in joining them and then purchased this heavily treed property. We began clearing some trees and settled into country life trying our hand at raising some turkeys and pigs as well as growing some hedging and conifers. We joined the Farmer's Market, then as our inventory expanded we began running a spec truck down island stopping here and there to show our quality product to potential customers.
Little by little the property was cleared and one by one the greenhouses and irrigation zones increased to what you see today. In the beginning we were limited to wholesale and landscape clientele, then somewhere along the way interest in our nursery grew and we opened to the public.

FAQ

The watering frequency for your plants depends on various factors such as the type of plant, the soil type, and the environmental conditions. As a general rule of thumb, most plants require watering when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch. However, it's essential to monitor your plants closely and adjust the watering frequency accordingly. Overwatering can lead to root rot, while underwatering can cause dehydration and wilting. We recommend checking the specific care instructions for each plant in your nursery to ensure optimal watering practices.

The ideal location for indoor plants varies depending on the plant species and its light requirements. In general, most indoor plants thrive in bright, indirect sunlight. Avoid placing plants in direct sunlight as it can scorch their leaves. Rooms with east or west-facing windows typically provide suitable light conditions for a wide range of indoor plants. Additionally, ensure adequate airflow around the plants to prevent issues such as fungal diseases and stagnant air. If you're unsure about the lighting needs of a particular plant, feel free to ask our nursery staff for guidance.

Preventing pests and diseases in your garden starts with practicing good plant care habits. Regularly inspect your plants for signs of pests such as aphids, spider mites, or scale insects. If you notice any pests, promptly remove them by hand or treat them with organic insecticidal soap. Additionally, avoid overwatering and ensure proper drainage to prevent fungal diseases such as powdery mildew and root rot. Maintaining good garden hygiene by removing dead or diseased plant material can also help prevent the spread of diseases. Finally, consider using natural predators such as ladybugs or introducing beneficial nematodes to control pest populations naturally. If you encounter persistent pest or disease issues, don't hesitate to seek advice from our nursery experts for personalized recommendations and solutions.
